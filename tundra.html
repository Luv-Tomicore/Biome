Savannah Grassland Biome

Location

Arctic Tundras are located in Northen Canada, edges of Alaska, Northen Russia,top edges of Antartica and around the edges of Greenland.

Climate

The climate of the Arctic Tundra is very harsh with very long winters,that last for 6-10 months, and short cool summers. In the winter the temperature ranges from a low of -34°C to a high of 12°C and it is very dry with strong winds. In the Tundra, the weather is so cold that the ground is permanently frozen below the surface, a layer called permafrost. It recives low percipitation and there can be no sunlight for as many as 163 days in a year. There is only about 50-60 days where the temperature is high enough for plants to grow.

Fauna (Animals)

In the Arctic Tundra, the majority of animals consist of carnivorous mammals, herbivorous mammals, fish and birds.

They include:

Herbivore Canivore
Lemmings Foxes
Artic hares Snow leopards
Squirrels Lynx
Finches
Wolves
Polar bears

Flora (Plants)

In the Arctic Tudra, there is a vast variety of flora (about 1700 different species)! Some of the most commonly found plants are; Cottton grass, Purple saxifrage, and the Arctic poppy. Another common plant in the Tundra as well as other places in the mountains, is Cranberry.
